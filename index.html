About Lavun Local Government

Learn about our history, wards, and educational institutions

History of Lavun LGA

Lavun Local Government Area was created in 1991 from the former Rijau Local Government. It is located in the northern part of Niger State, Nigeria, and has its headquarters in the town of Lavun.

The local government area covers an area of approximately 2,547 square kilometers and has a population of over 150,000 people according to the 2006 census. The predominant ethnic groups are the Hausa, Fulani, and Kamuku people.

Lavun LGA is known for its agricultural production, particularly of crops like maize, millet, sorghum, and groundnuts. The area also has significant mineral deposits including limestone and clay.

Founder of NALLGOS

Dr. Aminu Suleiman

Dr. Aminu Suleiman is the visionary founder of Lavun Student Association. Born and raised in Lavun LGA, he has dedicated his life to promoting education and youth development in the local government.

After completing his PhD in Education at Ahmadu Bello University, Zaria, Dr. Suleiman returned to Lavun with a mission to improve educational opportunities for students in the area. He established the NALLGOS in 2010 as a platform to unite students from the local government and provide them with academic support, mentorship, and scholarship opportunities.

Under his leadership, the association has awarded over 500 scholarships to deserving students and organized numerous educational programs and career guidance workshops. Dr. Suleiman's commitment to youth empowerment has earned him several awards and recognition at both state and national levels.

When not working on educational initiatives, Dr. Suleiman enjoys reading, mentoring young people, and promoting the cultural heritage of Lavun people.
